The 7 Problems I came up with in the gaming community

  1. A more serious issue, within the popular game minecraft, popular youtubers prey on children who don't know any better, and encourage them to take part in gambling. The children are watching videos where these youtubers promote their minecraft servers, all of which include an element of gambling, encouraging them to join in, lose money, and eventually become addicted. These youtubers make millions off of these poor kids, while they grow up to have a gambling addiction.
  2. The price for games has recently gone up, from $60 to $70, and nintendo took it a step further making Mario Kart World cost $80. With the way this is going, gaming is going to eventually turn from an expensive hobby to an impossible hobby. And that doesn't account for rammageddon. To explain, rammageddon is the stage of AI we are in now, where RAM has become a rare thing, and now is expensive because of AI using up all of the RAM. RAM is critical to gaming, and thus the price rising makes it harder to game. Overall the price of gaming has been rising as of late, which is something everyone suffers from.
  3. Finding time to game. What I mean is that in our busy lives, how are we supposed to find time to game? Working a 9 to 5, you work 8 hours, sleep 8 hours, and assuming you don't do over time or don't have ANY OTHER TASKS ( that really should be there ) you have 8 hours to game. But that's highly unrealistic. People have laundry, bills, travel time, personal hygiene, houses to organize,things to buy, and places to go. There's probably more but I think that's most of the important ones. You have to find time to play games in all of this, and when you do find time, its only like 30 minutes. Ask any gamer and they'll tell you they can't do anything with 30 minutes. I agree.
  4. People in the gaming community may struggle with staying healthy, as they may prefer sitting at their desks and playing games rather than exercise or going to the gym. What I mean by healthy is not morbidly obese, physically able ( as in able to do at least a few pushups ) and able to run at least a quarter mile without stopping.
  5. Gamers may struggle with keeping track of time, and stay up too late ( around 00:00 or even later ) to obtain healthy amounts of sleep, at least 8 hours. We all know the problem with missing sleep.
  6. Like I mentioned earlier, gaming is an expensive hobby, and it is hard to find the money to buy the thngs that you need for gaming. Gaming requires what is called "peripherals" which includes the screen, keyboard, desk, mouse, speakers, and just about everything you interface with.That doesn't even include the cost of the computer itself, which easily costs thousands for a low end computer. Peripherals are expensive ( a screen could cost hundreds ) and a bare minimum keyboard costs $50. In paying all of our bills and necessities, where do we find the money to pay these large prices?
  7. Stick drift. What is stick drift? Stick drift is where the joystick on your controller moves for you without you touching it, causing you to do unintentional moves and making it harder to game. Many controllers have this issue, and the money to replace a controller may not be there, or the controller is special, or maybe you don't want to replace the controller. This remains a common complaint of console gamers and pc gamers alike.
